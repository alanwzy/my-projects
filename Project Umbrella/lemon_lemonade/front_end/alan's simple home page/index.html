What is UV radiation?

Ultraviolet radiation (UVR) is a type of energy produced by the sun and some artificial sources.
UV radiation is the primary cause of skin cancer. Exposure to UV radiation frequently can cause significant damage to a child's skin and eyes and leads to serious impacts on their health conditions later in life such as Photokeratitis and Melanomas.
UV radiation can be high even on cool and overcast days. You can see sunlight and feel the tempreture but you can't measure UV radiation based on that. You are exposed to UV radiation directly from the sun no matter it is warm or cold and you won’t notice it until you have any skin/eye damage.

What is UV Index?

UV index is a measurement to indicate the level of UV radiation during a day. It is measured by the Australian Radiation Protection And Nuclear Safety Agency (ARPANSA).
The World Health Organization has defined the Global Solar UV Index to measure the UV levels on a scale from 0 (Low) to 11+ (Extreme). Sun protection is recommended when UV levels are 3 (Moderate) or higher.
