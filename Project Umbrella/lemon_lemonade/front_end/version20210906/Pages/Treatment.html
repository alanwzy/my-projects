First-aid Treatment:



How to treat a sunburn?
  • Take pain reliever tablets (would be better as gels)
  • Cool the skin with a tower damped in tap water several times per day.
  • Apply moisturizer or lotion to smooth the affected area.
  • Drink more water to prevent dehydration.
  • Do not try to break the blisters. If it does then apply antibiotic ointment and cover it up with a bandage.
  • Gently treat the affected area.
  • Protect your skin from any further exposure to sunlight.

You should see the doctor when:
  • The blister covers a large area of your body.
  • The swelling gets severe and does not improve after a few days.
  • The affected areas show signs of infection, such as Severe Pain or Red Fluid coming from the blisters.

Seek emergency medical care immediately when you have:
  • Fever
  • Faints
  • Dehydration
