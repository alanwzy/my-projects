What is Sunburn?

Sunburn is when your skin turns red, hot, and painful when touched. It appears within a couple of hours after intense exposure to UVR from the sunlight (or any other artificial sources).

Sunburn is the cause of dark spots and wrinkles on the skin. It also increases the risk of skin cancer.

Symptoms:

  • The skin turns pink or red.
  • The Skin feels warm or hot.
  • The Skin feels painful when touched.
  • The Skin swells up or has blisters.

Types of sunburns

First degree:
  • The outer layer of the skin is damaged.
  • Would heal by itself in a few days (from 3 to 6 days)

Symptoms:

  • The skin turns red and dry.
  • Swellings or Blisterings on the skin that are painful.
  • Peeling skin from 3 to 8 days after exposure.
Second degree:
  • The 2 outer layers of the skin are damaged.
  • Would leave scars.
  • Would take weeks to heal and require specialist treatment.

Symptoms:

  • The skin turns deep-red and shiny.
  • Painful swellings or blisterings on a large area of the skin.
  • The burned area turns white (discolored).

Note: Please seek medical treatment immediately if you have the Second degree of sunburn or worse.

Third degree:

When the skin is damaged more severely than the Second degree

Fourth degree:

Needs to be avoided at all costs

  • Not only the skin but also the fat and muscle underneath are damaged.
  • Would require surgical treatment.
  • Change the color of the entire skin.
