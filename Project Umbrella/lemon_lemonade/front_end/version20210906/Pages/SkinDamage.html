Why is UV Radiation(UVR) dangerous?

UVR is comprised of UVA and UVB rays:

  • UVA can deeply penetrate the skin and cause damages, such as wrinkling or blotchiness. UVA also suppresses our immune system.
  • UVB penetrates the top layer of the skin and causes sunburn, which could further lead to skin cancer

Frequent exposure to intense sunlight could cause premature aging of the skin as it becomes thicker and has wrinkles. Up to 90% of the visible skin aging changes are caused by the interaction with sunlight. With proper protection from UV radiation, most premature aging of the skin can be avoided.

If you see unusual skin growths on body areas that are frequently exposed to the sunlight, such as the face, hands, or neck, it could be a sign of skin disorders caused by UVR. Those areas could be raised, become red, or have a rough surface and also could increase the risk of having skin cancer. In that case, please come and see the doctor as soon as possible.

Skin cancer caused by UVR


UVR can damage the DNA of skin cells

Retrieve from: Cancer Council

Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that could be caused by UVR. It is one of the most common cancers among young people aging from 15 to 29. UV exposure and sunburns, particularly during childhood, increases the risk of having this disease.

Basal Cell Carcinomas is a type of skin cancer that could be caused by UVR. With this condition, small and fleshy bumps would appear on the head or neck before slowly growing larger. Those bumps could penetrate to the bone and cause significant damages.

Squamous Cell Carcinomas is similar to Basal Cell Carcinomas as red and scaly patches appear and spread on multiple parts of the body.
